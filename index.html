Tasmanian Bushfires

By Josh Duff

Tassie feels the heat

Tasmania is one of the most naturally diverse places in the world, teeming with native Australian and uniquely Tasmanian wildlife and flora. It hosts some of the cleanest air in the world and is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Australia for its beautiful national parks. Being a state dense with national parks and bush, Tasmania is a state very susceptible to bushfires. This endangers many native species each year and can wipe out entire tourist destinations, which significantly impacts Tasmania with tourism increasingly making the backbone of Tasmania's ecomomy.

Causes of bushfires

Most of Tasmanian bushfires can't be avoided, being ignited by natural events such as lightning strikes. A significant portion of these fires, however, are avoidable and started by human intervention, whether that be due to deliberate arson or accidental fires from leaving campfires anattended or losing control of bonfires.

Counter-intuitively, one of the best ways of reducing bushfires is with controlled burns. These planned burns are conducted to remove excess dry plant material, which could fuel potential bushfires. These controlled burns are carefully monitored and are conducted at times where temperatures are low and fires are much easier to control.

Several more of these bushfires have undetermined origins. These could be any of the above catagories, so there are likely many more fires being started due to human intervention.

Interactive Bushfire Map

The largest fires are generally the natural fires caused by lightening strikes. As seen in the map below, these fires normally occur in sparsely-inhabbited west and south-west regions. These are started generally by lightneing strikes and can becomes devestatingly big due to the difficulty in accessing these fires deep in bushland to be able to extinguish them.

Most accidental fires can be seen on the east-coast, where many popular camping spots can be found. This indicates people leaving campfires unattended or letting bonfires go out of control.
